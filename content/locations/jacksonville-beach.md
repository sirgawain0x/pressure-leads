## The Coastal Challenge: Why Beach Properties Need More Frequent Cleaning

Properties within 5 miles of the Atlantic Ocean face environmental factors that accelerate surface deterioration:

### Salt Air Deposits
Airborne salt particles settle on every exterior surface — siding, roofing, concrete, and metal. Salt is hygroscopic, meaning it attracts and holds moisture, creating a perpetually damp environment for mold and algae. Salt also corrodes metal hardware, gutters, and flashing faster than inland locations.

### Sand Abrasion
Windblown sand acts like fine sandpaper on painted surfaces and siding. It embeds in porous concrete and between paver joints, making surfaces rough and harder to clean over time.

### Intensified UV Exposure
Reflective surfaces (sand, water) amplify UV radiation on beachfront properties. This accelerates paint fading, wood graying, and the breakdown of protective coatings.

### Higher Humidity and Salt Fog
The ocean creates a persistent marine layer that keeps surfaces damp longer, especially on east-facing walls. This extended moisture exposure promotes faster biological growth.

For these reasons, we recommend Jacksonville Beach homeowners clean their exteriors **every 8–12 months** rather than the annual schedule sufficient for inland properties.
