## Why Palm Coast Properties Get Dirty Fast

Palm Coast's natural beauty comes with maintenance challenges:

### Heavy Tree Canopy
Palm Coast was developed to preserve its natural tree coverage. While the live oaks, pines, and palms provide shade and beauty, they also:
- Block sunlight, keeping surfaces damp and promoting mold growth
- Drop pollen, leaves, and tannin-rich debris that stain concrete and siding
- Create habitats for algae, lichen, and moss on roofs and north-facing walls

### Canal and Waterfront Exposure
Many Palm Coast homes sit along the city's extensive canal system or the Intracoastal Waterway. The proximity to water increases ambient humidity, accelerating biological growth on exterior surfaces. Canal-side homes often have greener, moldier exteriors than properties even a block inland.

### Sandy Soil and Irrigation
Palm Coast's sandy soil means more irrigation is needed for landscaping. Sprinkler overspray hits siding, fences, and driveways daily, depositing minerals, iron stains, and keeping surfaces constantly damp.

### Coastal Proximity
While Palm Coast isn't directly on the beach, properties east of I-95 experience moderate salt air exposure from the Atlantic, particularly during northeast wind events.
