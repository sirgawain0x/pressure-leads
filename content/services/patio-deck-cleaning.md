## Why Florida Patios and Decks Get So Dirty

Northeast Florida's climate creates a perfect storm for outdoor surface deterioration:

- **90%+ humidity** year-round feeds mold and mildew growth
- **Frequent afternoon rain showers** keep surfaces consistently damp
- **Tree pollen and oak debris** from live oaks, palms, and pine trees stain and clog surface pores
- **Tannin stains** from fallen leaves create dark brown discoloration
- **Salt air** (for coastal properties) accelerates weathering and oxidation
- **Intense UV exposure** fades and dries out wood, causing graying and cracking

Without regular cleaning, these factors compound year after year, making restoration increasingly difficult and expensive.

## Our Patio & Deck Cleaning Process

We tailor our approach to your specific surface material:

### For Wood Decks
1. **Assessment** — We identify the wood type (pressure-treated pine, cedar, ipe, etc.) and evaluate its condition
2. **Pre-treatment** — A wood-safe cleaning solution is applied to kill mold and mildew without damaging wood fibers
3. **Controlled pressure washing** — We use moderate pressure (1,200–1,500 PSI) with a fan tip to clean wood surfaces without splintering or etching
4. **Brightening treatment** — An oxalic acid-based wood brightener restores the wood's natural color and opens the grain for better stain absorption
5. **Optional staining/sealing** — We can apply protective stain or sealant after cleaning (recommended for maximum longevity)

### For Concrete Patios
1. **Degreaser application** — Treats oil, grease, and food stains from grills and outdoor cooking
2. **Surface cleaning** — Commercial surface cleaner delivers uniform results at 3,000+ PSI
3. **Detail work** — Edges, corners, and expansion joints cleaned thoroughly
4. **Post-treatment** — Mildewcide applied to slow regrowth

### For Pavers
1. **Pre-rinse and weed removal** — Loose debris and weeds between joints are cleared
2. **Low-to-moderate pressure cleaning** — Adjusted settings protect paver integrity and joint sand
3. **Re-sanding** — Polymeric sand applied to joints after cleaning to prevent weed regrowth and ant hills
4. **Optional sealing** — Paver sealant locks in color and provides a protective barrier

## How Often Should You Clean Your Patio or Deck?

In Northeast Florida, we recommend:

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will pressure washing damage my wood deck?
Not when done correctly. We use lower pressure settings and wider spray patterns specifically calibrated for wood surfaces. Our technicians are trained to clean with the grain and maintain a consistent distance to prevent etching or splintering.

### Can you clean my deck without chemicals?
Pressure alone can remove surface dirt, but it won't kill mold and mildew at the root. Without a cleaning solution, growth returns within weeks. Our biodegradable, wood-safe solutions ensure a deeper, longer-lasting clean.

### Should I seal my deck after pressure washing?
We strongly recommend it. Cleaning opens the wood grain and removes old sealant, leaving the wood unprotected. Applying a quality stain or sealant within 24–48 hours of cleaning provides the best protection against Florida's elements.

### Can you clean composite decking like Trex?
Yes. Composite decking requires lower pressure and different cleaning solutions than wood. We follow manufacturer guidelines to clean composite surfaces safely and effectively.

### Do you clean pool decks?
Absolutely. Pool decks collect algae, sunscreen residue, and mineral deposits from splash-out. We clean all pool deck surfaces including concrete, pavers, travertine, and cool deck coatings.
