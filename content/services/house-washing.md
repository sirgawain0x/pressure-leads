## Soft Wash vs. Pressure Wash: Why It Matters for Your Home

Many homeowners don't realize that high-pressure washing can actually damage house siding. Standard pressure washing at 3,000+ PSI can:

- Force water behind vinyl and hardie board siding, causing moisture damage
- Strip paint and protective coatings from wood surfaces
- Crack or chip stucco and EIFS systems
- Break window seals, leading to foggy double-pane glass
- Damage caulking around windows and doors

That's why we use the **soft wash method** for all house exteriors. Soft washing uses low pressure (under 500 PSI — similar to a garden hose) combined with specialized cleaning solutions that kill mold, mildew, and algae at the cellular level. The result is a deeper, longer-lasting clean that's completely safe for every exterior material.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is soft washing safe for my plants and landscaping?
Yes. We pre-rinse all landscaping before and after the wash. Our cleaning solutions are biodegradable and break down quickly. We've washed thousands of homes without landscaping damage.

### Will house washing remove paint from my siding?
No. Soft washing uses low pressure that won't strip sound paint. If paint is already peeling or failing, those loose flakes may come off — which actually helps identify areas that need repainting.

### Can you wash a two-story or three-story home?
Absolutely. We have equipment that safely reaches up to three stories without ladders, ensuring consistent coverage and a safe work environment.

### How long does house washing take?
Most single-family homes take 1.5–3 hours depending on size and condition. The cleaning solution needs dwell time to work effectively, so we don't rush the process.

### What's the difference between house washing and roof cleaning?
House washing addresses vertical siding surfaces using soft wash. Roof cleaning is a separate service that treats shingles, tile, or metal roofing. We recommend both for complete exterior maintenance.
