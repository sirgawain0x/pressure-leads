## How Florida's Climate Attacks Your Fence

Your fence faces more environmental stress than almost any other structure on your property:

- **Constant ground-level moisture** from sprinklers, rain, and morning dew creates ideal conditions for mold and mildew
- **Direct soil contact** on fence posts accelerates rot and attracts termites
- **Intense UV radiation** breaks down wood fibers and fades stain color within 1–2 years
- **Algae and lichen** colonize shaded fence sections, especially along tree lines
- **Pollen accumulation** from live oaks, pines, and palms coats fence surfaces each spring
- **Salt air exposure** (in coastal areas) corrodes metal hardware and accelerates wood deterioration

Regular professional cleaning prevents these issues from becoming permanent damage.

## Our Fence Cleaning Process

1. **Material assessment** — We identify your fence material, condition, and any damage that needs attention before cleaning.
2. **Pre-treatment** — A material-appropriate cleaning solution is applied to kill mold, mildew, and algae. For wood, we use an oxygen bleach-based cleaner that's safe for plants and pets. For vinyl, we use a soft wash solution.
3. **Cleaning** — Wood fences are cleaned with controlled pressure (800–1,500 PSI) using a fan tip. Vinyl and aluminum fences receive a soft wash rinse at low pressure.
4. **Brightening (wood only)** — An oxalic acid brightener restores wood to its natural warm tone and neutralizes any remaining cleaning solution.
5. **Inspection and touch-up** — We check both sides of the fence (where accessible) and hand-clean any missed spots around posts, gates, and hardware.
6. **Optional staining/sealing** — We recommend staining or sealing wood fences within 24–48 hours of cleaning for maximum protection and color retention.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will pressure washing damage my wood fence?
Not with our approach. We use lower pressure settings and fan tips specifically designed for wood surfaces. Our technicians maintain consistent distance and pressure to clean effectively without splintering, gouging, or etching the wood.

### Can you clean both sides of my fence?
We clean both sides whenever accessible. If the opposite side faces a neighbor's property, we'll coordinate with you on access. Many homeowners arrange cleaning with their neighbors to share the cost.

### Should I stain my fence after cleaning?
For wood fences, absolutely. Cleaning removes the old stain and opens the wood grain, leaving it exposed to UV and moisture. Applying a quality stain or sealant within 48 hours of cleaning provides the best adhesion and longest-lasting protection.

### How do you protect my landscaping during fence cleaning?
We pre-rinse all plants, shrubs, and grass along the fence line before applying cleaning solutions. Our cleaners are biodegradable, and we do a thorough post-rinse of all vegetation after cleaning. We've cleaned thousands of fence lines without landscaping damage.

### Can you remove green algae stains from vinyl fencing?
Yes. Our soft wash solution is specifically formulated to eliminate algae, mold, and mildew from vinyl surfaces. Most vinyl fences look brand-new after a single cleaning.
