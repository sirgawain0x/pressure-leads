## Why Your Driveway Needs Professional Pressure Washing

Florida's subtropical climate is tough on driveways. Here's what builds up on your surfaces over time:

- **Oil and automotive fluid stains** from parked vehicles that seep into porous concrete
- **Black mold and mildew** that thrive in Florida's warm, humid conditions
- **Green algae growth** especially in shaded areas near trees and landscaping
- **Tire marks and rubber deposits** that regular hosing can't remove
- **Rust stains** from irrigation systems, fertilizer, and metal furniture

Standard garden hoses and store-bought cleaners barely scratch the surface. Our commercial pressure washing equipment delivers 3,000–4,000 PSI of cleaning power combined with hot water and eco-friendly detergents that break down even the most stubborn stains at the molecular level.

## Our Driveway Cleaning Process

We don't just blast your driveway with water and call it done. Our proven four-step process delivers consistent, thorough results every time:

1. **Pre-treatment** — We apply a professional-grade degreaser and mold-killing solution to the entire surface, letting it dwell for 10–15 minutes to loosen embedded grime.
2. **Surface cleaning** — Using a commercial surface cleaner attachment (not just a wand), we clean the driveway in overlapping passes. This eliminates the streaky "zebra stripe" pattern that amateur pressure washing leaves behind.
3. **Edge and detail work** — We hand-clean expansion joints, edges along the garage, and areas around landscaping borders where dirt accumulates most.
4. **Post-treatment rinse** — A final rinse removes all loosened debris, and we apply a mildewcide treatment that helps keep your driveway cleaner for longer.

## How Often Should You Pressure Wash Your Driveway in Florida?

We recommend pressure washing your driveway **at least once per year** in Northeast Florida. Homes with heavy tree coverage, north-facing driveways, or proximity to the coast may benefit from cleaning every 6–8 months. Regular maintenance prevents permanent staining and extends the life of your concrete or pavers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will pressure washing damage my driveway?
No. Our technicians are trained to use the correct pressure settings and nozzle tips for each surface type. We adjust our approach for delicate materials like stamped concrete and pavers to deliver a deep clean without causing damage.

### How long does driveway pressure washing take?
Most standard two-car driveways take 45–90 minutes. Larger driveways or heavily stained surfaces may take up to two hours.

### Can you remove oil stains from my driveway?
Yes. We use commercial-grade degreasers specifically designed for petroleum-based stains. While some very old, deeply penetrated oil stains may not come out 100%, we achieve dramatic improvement in virtually every case.

### Do I need to be home during the service?
No. As long as we have access to a water spigot and the driveway area, we can complete the work while you're away. We'll send before and after photos when finished.

### How soon can I drive on my driveway after cleaning?
You can walk on it immediately and drive on it within 1–2 hours once the surface is fully dry.
